## Why Focus on Muscle Preservation?

Summer is the peak season for achieving body composition goals. However, blind caloric restriction and excessive cardio often lead to muscle loss, which lowers your basal metabolic rate and leaves your physique looking soft. To maintain muscle during a cut, you must follow a structured training and nutrition protocol.

## Core Strategy 1: Moderate Caloric Deficit

Fat loss does not mean starvation. A large caloric deficit triggers catabolism, causing the body to prioritize the breakdown of muscle tissue.

* Set the Deficit: Aim for a daily intake of 300 to 500 calories below your maintenance level.
* Rate of Loss: Losing 0.5% to 1% of your body weight per week is the optimal pace to maximize muscle retention.

## Core Strategy 2: Maintain High-Intensity Resistance Training

A common mistake is shifting focus entirely to long-duration cardio while neglecting weightlifting.

* Maintain Intensity: Attempt to keep your training loads heavy. Providing sufficient mechanical tension tells your body that your muscles are still needed, which is the best defense against muscle wasting.
* Compound Movements: Focus on heavy compound lifts (Squats, Deadlifts, Bench Press). Even if your total training volume decreases slightly, maintain the intensity.

## Core Strategy 3: Prioritize Protein Intake

When calories are restricted, protein is your primary muscle-sparing nutrient.

* Recommended Intake: Consume 1.8g to 2.2g of protein per kilogram of body weight daily.
* High-Quality Sources: Choose chicken breast, fish, lean beef, eggs, and whey protein.
* Frequency: Distribute protein intake across 4 to 5 meals throughout the day to keep muscle protein synthesis elevated.

## Core Strategy 4: Moderate Cardio

Cardio is a tool for fat loss, but it should not be the primary driver.

* Minimalist Approach: Schedule 2 to 3 sessions per week, each consisting of 30 minutes of Low-Intensity Steady State (LISS) cardio, such as brisk walking.
* Avoid Overtraining: Excessive HIIT or long-duration cardio can hinder recovery from resistance training and increase the risk of muscle breakdown.

## Conclusion

Effective fat loss is a marathon, not a sprint. By prioritizing protein, maintaining resistance training intensity, and managing your caloric deficit, you can achieve a lean and defined physique by the peak of summer. Remember, patience is the best supplement during a cut.
