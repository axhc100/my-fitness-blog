## Body Recomposition Guide

Many people believe:

**"You must lose muscle to lose fat, and gain fat to build muscle."**

That is not always true.

With the right combination of training, nutrition, and recovery, many lifters can:

- Reduce body fat
- Build lean muscle
- Increase strength
- Improve body shape

This process is known as:

**Body Recomposition.**

## What Is Body Recomposition?

Body recomposition means:

Building or maintaining muscle while reducing body fat.

Traditional approaches separate goals:

Fat loss phase:

Lose weight

Bulking phase:

Gain weight

Body recomposition focuses on:

**Improving body composition, not just scale weight.**

## Who Benefits Most?

### Beginners

New lifters often experience rapid improvements because their bodies respond strongly to training.

### Returning Lifters

Muscle memory allows faster recovery of previous muscle size and strength.

### People With Higher Body Fat

Stored energy can support muscle-building adaptations.

## The Core Principles

## 1. High-Quality Strength Training

Resistance training is the foundation.

Focus on:

- Progressive overload
- Compound movements
- Effective training volume
- Proper technique

Key exercises:

- Squats
- Deadlifts
- Bench Press
- Rows
- Overhead Press

## 2. Manage Calories

Extreme dieting is unnecessary.

A moderate calorie deficit works well:

Approximately 200–400 calories below maintenance.

Benefits:

- Maintain training performance
- Reduce fat
- Support recovery

## 3. Prioritize Protein

Protein supports muscle repair and growth.

Recommended:

1.6–2.2g/kg body weight daily

Great sources:

- Chicken
- Beef
- Eggs
- Fish
- Dairy
- Legumes

## Training Structure

A good approach:

4–5 strength sessions per week.

Example:

### Monday

Chest + Triceps

### Tuesday

Back + Biceps

### Wednesday

Rest or low-intensity cardio

### Thursday

Leg Training

### Friday

Shoulders + Core

### Saturday

Functional Training or Cardio

### Sunday

Recovery

## Cardio During Recomposition

Moderate cardio helps:

- Increase calorie expenditure
- Improve cardiovascular fitness
- Enhance recovery

Recommended:

2–4 sessions weekly

20–40 minutes each

Good options:

- Walking
- Cycling
- Elliptical

## How To Measure Progress

Don't rely only on body weight.

Track:

- Waist measurement
- Muscle size
- Strength levels
- Body fat percentage
- Visual changes

Sometimes the scale stays the same while your body improves dramatically.

## Common Mistakes

### Extreme Calorie Restriction

Too little food reduces performance.

### Only Doing Cardio

Without resistance training, muscle retention becomes difficult.

### Daily Weight Obsession

Water changes can hide real progress.

## Recovery Matters

Successful recomposition requires:

- 7–9 hours of sleep
- Enough protein
- Stress management
- Regular deload periods

Training creates the signal.

Recovery creates the transformation.

## Final Thoughts

Body recomposition is not about simply becoming lighter.

It is about building a stronger, leaner, healthier body.

With:

**Strength Training + High Protein Nutrition + Smart Calories + Recovery**

you can transform your physique while improving performance.

**The ultimate fitness goal is not just losing weight—it is becoming stronger, healthier, and more capable.**
